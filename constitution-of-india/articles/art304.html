PART XIII (TRADE, COMMERCE AND INTERCOURSE WITHIN THE TERRITORY OF INDIA) >>

Article 304. Restrictions on trade, commerce and intercourse among States

  1. Notwithstanding anything in article 301 or article 303, the Legislature of a State may by lawâ€”
    1. (a) impose on goods imported from other States 1[or the Union territories] any tax to which similar goods manufactured or produced in that State are subject, so, however, as not to discriminate between goods so imported and goods so manufactured or produced; and
    2. (b) impose such reasonable restrictions on the freedom of trade, commerce or intercourse with or within that State as may be required in the public interest:
  2. Provided that no Bill or amendment for the purposes of clause (b) shall be introduced or moved in the Legislature of a State without the previous sanction of the President.
  1. [1] Ins. by the Constitution (Seventh Amendment) Act, 1956, s. 29 and Sch. (w.e.f. 1-11-1956).
